GalaxyPepDock peptide-HLA docking was performed for six selected non-toxic T-cell epitopes, including three MHC-I epitopes and three MHC-II epitopes. All selected peptides produced acceptable top-ranked peptide-HLA complex models. The MHC-I docking models showed stronger interaction similarity scores and estimated accuracy values compared with the MHC-II models. The best docking result was observed for the ompK36-derived MHC-I epitope KYVDVGATY docked with HLA-A*24:02, with an interaction similarity score of 254.0 and estimated accuracy of 1.000. Overall, docking supported the structural compatibility of the selected epitopes with their corresponding HLA receptors. Docking was interpreted as supportive computational validation, not as experimental proof of binding.
